Mercury

Materials: Large iron core, silicate mantle and crust

Size: 2440 kilometers

Mass: 3.3 x 10^23 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~58,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: 167 degrees Celsius

Description: Closest planet to the sun and smallest in our solar system, Mercury was named after the ancient Roman god of Commerce and Communication, Mercurius

Venus

Materials: Crust made mostly of basaltic rock and a Core likely composed of iron and nickel

Size: 12,104 kilometers

Mass: 4.87 x 10^24 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~108,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: 465 degrees Celsius

Description: Hottest planet in our solar system and similar in size and weight to planet Earth, Venus was named after the ancient Roman goddess of Love, Venus

Earth

Materials: Crust primarily composed of silicate minerals, such as quartz and feldspar, and its Core is mainly iron and nickel

Size: 12,760 kilometers

Mass: 5.97 x 10^24 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~149,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: 15 degrees Celsius

Description: Planet of life, the name Earth was taken from old English and Germanic, which means 'the ground'

Mars

Materials: Crust made of basaltic rock and a Core that is likely composed of iron, nickel, and sulfur

Size: 6779 kilometers

Mass: 6.42 x 10^23 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~228,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: -60 degrees Celsius

Description: The Red planet, Mars was named after the ancient Roman god of war, Mars

Jupiter

Materials: A gas giant with a composition primarily of hydrogen and helium and a Core that contains elements like rock and metallic hydrogen

Size: 142,984 kilometers

Mass: 1.9 x 10^27 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~778,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: -110 degrees Celsius

Description: Known for the Great Red Spot and being the Biggest planet in our solar system, Jupiter was named after the ancient Roman god of the sky and thunder, and king of the gods in ancient Roman religion, Jupiter

Saturn

Materials: Similar to Jupiter, Saturn is a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with a core of rock and metallic hydrogen

Size: 120,500 kilometers

Mass: 5.69 x 10^26 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~1,400,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: -178 degrees Celsius

Description: Known for its marvelous Rings and having the most Moons in our solar system, Saturn was named after the ancient Roman god of time, wealth, and agriculture

Uranus

Materials: An ice giant with a composition that includes water, ammonia, and methane 'ices', as well as hydrogen and helium

Size: 51,118 kilometers

Mass: 8.68 x 10^25 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~2,900,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: -195 degrees Celsius

Description: Rotating at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit, Uranus was named after the ancient Greek god of the sky, Uranus

Neptune

Materials: Neptune is an ice giant with a similar composition to Uranus, consisting of water, ammonia, and methane ices, along with hydrogen and helium

Size: 49,528 kilometers

Mass: 1.02 x 10^26 kilograms

Average Distance from the Sun: ~4,500,000,000 kilometers

Average Surface Temperature: -214 degrees Celsius

Description: Being the coldest and farthest planet from the Sun, Neptune was named after the ancient Roman god of the sea, Neptune
